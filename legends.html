Mahmoud Elkhatib


Al-Khatib was born in an Egyptian village located in the center of Sinbillawain in Dakahlia Governorate. From the village, the little boy moved with his family to Cairo. Mahmoud Al-Khatib joined Al-Nasr Club in Heliopolis, and when he was fifteen years old, his name became frequent in the field of football, especially during his participation in the schools league with the sports school. Al-Nasr Club officials agreed to transfer Al-Khatib to Al-Ahly Club after he was close to signing for Al-Ismaily, and Al-Khatib was only 16 years old at the time.

At this age, Al-Khatib played with the U-18 team, and his first match was against his former club, Al-Nasr. He joined the first team to play his first official match against the Plastic Club on October 15, 1972. In the same match, he scored his first goal, and after that, the goals continued in the league until, on the day of his retirement, he reached 108 goals in the league championship, which he scored in 199 matches during 17 continuous years of playing.

In his sixteen years with the Red Devils, Al-Khatib won 10 league titles and 5 Egyptian cups. He also made history in continental competitions, scoring 37 goals in 49 matches, a record that has not been surpassed yet. With Al-Ahly, Al-Khatib lifted the African Champion Clubs Cup (the predecessor of the African Champions League) in 1982 and 1987, and the African Cup Winners' Cup in 1984, 1985, and 1986. On the international scene, Al-Khatib made his debut with the national team in 1974 and participated with the Pharaohs in the 1980 and 1984 Olympic Games. He won the African Cup of Nations in 1986. Al-Khatib won individual titles, most notably the Best African Player in 1983 from France Football magazine, in addition to the title of top scorer in the Egyptian League in the 1977-78 and 1980-81 seasons. He is a member of the 100 Club list in the Egyptian League, in fifth place with (109 goals).

Mohamed Abo Treka


Mohamed Mohamed Mohamed Aboutrika (born November 7, 1978) is a former Egyptian international footballer who played as an attacking midfielder and second striker. He is known for being a playmaker and contributing to the attack and scoring goals, in addition to his magical touches, dribbling and penetration skills, as his playing style has been compared to that of the famous French player Zinedine Zidane. He began his football career playing with Tersana Club as a junior, then moved to the club's first team and was able to advance to the Egyptian Premier League and played with them for 4 seasons, then moved to Al-Ahly Club and was able to achieve a series of consecutive championships and achievements locally and internationally, most notably the bronze medal in the Club World Cup in 2006, where he achieved the title of top scorer at the time, in addition to 5 African Champions League championships in the years: 2005, 2006, 2008, 2012, 2013, and 4 African Super Cups. He also won 7 league championships, 3 cups, and 4 local Super Cups with Al-Ahly Club. In 2012, he moved to Bani Yas on loan, where he won the Gulf Club Football Championship with them. He then returned to Al-Ahly in 2013 and was able to win the 2013 African Champions League for the fifth time with Al-Ahly. After the end of the tournament, he announced that the Club World Cup would be his final destination in the world of football.

He started playing internationally in 2001 with the Egyptian national football team and played in the 2006 FIFA World Cup qualifiers as a regular player for the first time, then the 2006 African Cup of Nations where he began to shine when he scored two goals and scored the last penalty kick that contributed to the Egyptian team winning the championship, then winning the same championship in 2008 for the second time in a row, then played in the 2009 FIFA Confederations Cup and was able with his team to give a good performance in the championship, including the victory over the Italian team, the world champion at the time.

Aboutrika has won the CAF award for the best African player in the continent four times, and he holds the record for it. Aboutrika is still the top scorer in the history of the African Champions League with 33 goals, as well as the top scorer in the Cairo Derby (the summit match) with 13 goals. He is also one of the FIFA Centenary Club and one of the Hundred Club with 105 goals. In 2014, Aboutrika was chosen by FIFA as one of the best players in the history of the Club World Cup, and his goal in Hiroshima was chosen as the best goal in the history of the Club World Cup. In 2014, CAF chose Aboutrika as an ambassador for African football. In January 2016, Aboutrika was classified among the list of football legends by the International Federation of Football History and Statistics. In May 2016, he was honored by FIFA among a group of football legends at the 66th FIFA Congress in Mexico.

Aboutrika won many individual titles at the international, African, and local levels during his football career, which made him one of the most prominent players in the history of Egyptian, African, and Arab football and one of the legends in the history of Al-Ahly Club, before he officially announced his retirement from playing in December 2013. After his retirement, Aboutrika worked as a sports analyst on beIN Sports channels since the 2014 World Cup until now.
